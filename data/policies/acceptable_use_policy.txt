ACME CORPORATION — IT SECURITY POLICY
Document:  Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
Version:   4.2
Effective: January 1, 2025
Owner:     IT Security / Legal
Review:    Annually (next review: January 2026)
Classification: Internal

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This policy defines acceptable use of ACME's information technology
resources to protect company data, maintain system integrity, and
ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Applies to all employees, contractors, interns, and third parties
using ACME-owned, ACME-managed, or ACME-leased IT resources including
computers, mobile devices, network infrastructure, software, email,
and cloud services.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. PERMITTED USE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1 Primary purpose
   ACME IT resources are provided for legitimate business purposes.
   Use must be consistent with job responsibilities.

3.2 Incidental personal use
   Limited personal use of ACME resources is permitted provided it:
   • Does not interfere with work duties or system performance.
   • Does not violate any provision of this policy.
   • Is not for commercial gain or operating a personal business.
   • Does not involve streaming media services, online gaming, or
     large file downloads during business hours.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The following are strictly prohibited on ACME IT resources:

4.1 Security violations
   • Attempting to gain unauthorised access to any system or network.
   • Installing, running, or distributing malicious software.
   • Circumventing, disabling, or tampering with any security control
     (firewalls, AV, DLP, MFA) — see MFA Policy §7 for exception process.
   • Intercepting network traffic or performing port scans without
     written IT Security authorisation.
   • Using ACME systems to conduct denial-of-service attacks.

4.2 Data & IP violations
   • Sharing, copying, or transmitting proprietary data without
     authorisation (see Data Classification Policy v2.1).
   • Downloading or distributing pirated software, music, video, or
     other copyrighted material.
   • Using ACME systems to misappropriate intellectual property.

4.3 Identity & credential violations
   • Sharing login credentials with any person.
   • Accessing another employee's accounts or files without explicit
     written authorisation from IT Security and the account owner.
   • Impersonating another user or creating fake identities.

4.4 Content violations
   • Accessing, storing, or transmitting content that is illegal,
     obscene, discriminatory, harassing, or threatening.
   • Participating in activities that constitute fraud or deceit.

4.5 External sharing violations
   • Sending Confidential or Restricted data outside ACME via personal
     email, consumer cloud storage (Dropbox, personal Google Drive), or
     USB drives without DLP approval.
   • Approved external collaboration tools: SharePoint, OneDrive,
     ACME-managed Box account.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. MONITORING & PRIVACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • ACME reserves the right to monitor all activity on company-owned
     devices and networks.
   • Employees have NO expectation of privacy on company IT resources.
   • Monitoring includes: email, web browsing, file access, application
     usage, and network traffic (DLP).
   • Monitoring is conducted in accordance with applicable laws and
     is disclosed to employees upon hiring and annually thereafter.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. BRING YOUR OWN DEVICE (BYOD)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Personal devices accessing ACME email or systems must be enrolled
     in Intune MDM (enroll via https://portal.company.com/mdm).
   • MDM enrollment installs Intune Company Portal; personal data is
     NOT accessed or deleted during device management.
   • If a BYOD device is lost/stolen, report immediately to IT so
     corporate data can be remotely wiped (corporate partition only).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. SOCIAL MEDIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Employees must not disclose confidential company information on
     social media platforms.
   • ACME's Social Media Policy (separate document) governs public
     commentary about the company.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. REPORTING VIOLATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Report suspected violations to: compliance@company.com or
     the IT Security team (soc@company.com).
   • Anonymous reporting: https://ethics.company.com/report
   • Retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. ENFORCEMENT & CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Violations may result in:
   • Formal warning
   • Suspension of IT access privileges
   • Disciplinary action, up to and including termination
   • Legal action where applicable
