Classification: Internal

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Define a consistent framework for classifying, labelling, and handling
ACME data to ensure appropriate protection proportional to sensitivity
and business risk.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All ACME data regardless of format (digital, physical, verbal) and all
persons who create, access, process, or dispose of that data.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. DATA CLASSIFICATION LEVELS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1 PUBLIC
   Definition: Information approved for release to the general public.
   Examples:   Press releases, public product documentation, job postings,
               marketing materials.
   Handling:   No special controls required. May be shared freely.

3.2 INTERNAL
   Definition: General business information not intended for external
               audiences but not highly sensitive.
   Examples:   Internal memos, project plans, IT procedures, org charts,
               incident response logs (redacted).
   Handling:
     • Store on company-approved systems (SharePoint, OneDrive).
     • Do not post publicly or email to personal accounts.
     • Dispose via standard recycle bin / file deletion.

3.3 CONFIDENTIAL
   Definition: Sensitive business information that could harm ACME or
               individuals if disclosed.
   Examples:   Personally Identifiable Information (PII) — names, email
               addresses, phone numbers, employee IDs, date of birth,
               national ID / SSN (last 4 acceptable in verbal verification
               only — never in writing or logs).
               Customer data, contracts, financial forecasts, HR records,
               security vulnerability details, authentication credentials.
   Handling:
     • Encrypt at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
     • Access on need-to-know basis with manager approval.
     • Do not include in email bodies — use secure links (SharePoint).
     • Redact PII before sharing incident logs externally.
     • Physical copies: shred when no longer needed.
     • Dispose via secure-delete tools (not regular trash).

3.4 RESTRICTED
   Definition: Highly sensitive information with significant legal,
               regulatory, or financial consequences if disclosed.
   Examples:   M&A deal details, trade secrets, source code (core IP),
               financial statements (pre-release), security keys/certificates,
               SOC 2 audit reports (pending), CISO-only security assessments.
   Handling:
     • Access requires written CISO or VP-level approval.
     • Multi-factor authentication mandatory for all systems holding data.
     • Encryption at rest (AES-256) + hardware security modules (HSMs)
       for key material.
     • Audit log of every access event retained for 2 years.
     • Do not discuss on unencrypted communication channels.
     • Physical copies stored in locked cabinets; access log maintained.

5. PII HANDLING RULES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5.1 Collection
   • Collect only data necessary for the business purpose (data minimisation).
   • Obtain consent where required by GDPR / CCPA.

5.2 Storage
   • Do not store PII in plain-text log files.
   • Pseudonymise or anonymise where feasible.
   • Retention: 3 years post-employment for HR records, per legal guidance.

5.3 Transmission
   • Encrypt all PII in transit (TLS 1.2+).
   • Never transmit SSN, full credit card numbers, or passwords via email.

5.4 Disposal
   • Digital: NIST 800-88-compliant secure erasure or cryptographic erase.
   • Physical: Cross-cut shredding.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • GDPR (EU/UK): Article 5 — data minimisation; Article 32 — security.
   • CCPA (California): Right to know, delete, and opt-out.
   • SOC 2 Type II: CC6 series controls for logical access.
   • HIPAA: Not currently in scope; if healthcare data is processed,
     notify Compliance immediately.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. LABELLING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Documents must include classification in the header/footer.
   • Emails: prepend subject with [CONFIDENTIAL] or [RESTRICTED] as applicable.
   • Automated DLP labels applied by Microsoft Purview (MIP).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. VIOLATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Mishandling of Confidential or Restricted data must be reported to
   the DLP team (dlp@company.com) within 24 hours of discovery.
   Data breaches follow escalation in Incident Response Procedure v2.0.
